# Exercise Knowledge Base

## Cardio Exercises

### Walking
- Low impact, suitable for all fitness levels
- Start with 20-30 minutes, gradually increase duration
- Maintain moderate pace where you can hold a conversation
- Great for beginners and recovery days

### Running/Jogging
- Higher intensity cardio exercise
- Start with intervals: 1 min run, 2 min walk
- Gradually increase running time
- Requires proper running shoes and form
- Avoid if you have knee or joint issues

### Cycling
- Low impact on joints
- Can be done indoors or outdoors
- Adjust resistance for intensity
- Good for building leg strength and endurance

### Swimming
- Full body, low impact exercise
- Excellent for people with joint issues
- Works cardiovascular system and muscles
- Requires access to pool

## Strength Exercises

### Bodyweight Squats
- Targets: Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings
- Form: Feet shoulder-width, lower as if sitting in chair
- Beginners: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
- Progression: Add weight or increase reps

### Push-ups
- Targets: Chest, shoulders, triceps, core
- Form: Keep body straight, lower until chest nearly touches ground
- Beginners: Start with knee push-ups or wall push-ups
- Progression: Standard push-ups, then variations

### Plank
- Targets: Core muscles
- Form: Straight line from head to heels
- Hold for 20-60 seconds
- Progression: Increase hold time or add variations

### Lunges
- Targets: Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings
- Form: Step forward, lower back knee toward ground
- Keep front knee over ankle
- 3 sets of 10 reps per leg

### Deadlifts (with proper form)
- Targets: Hamstrings, glutes, back
- Requires proper technique to avoid injury
- Start with light weight or bodyweight
- Consult trainer for form guidance

## Flexibility and Mobility

### Stretching
- Perform after workouts when muscles are warm
- Hold stretches for 20-30 seconds
- Don't bounce - use static stretching
- Focus on major muscle groups used

### Yoga
- Improves flexibility, strength, and balance
- Can be adapted to all fitness levels
- Good for stress relief and recovery
- Many online resources available

## Exercise Safety

### Warm-up
- 5-10 minutes of light cardio
- Dynamic stretching
- Prepares body for exercise
- Reduces injury risk

### Cool-down
- 5-10 minutes of light activity
- Static stretching
- Helps with recovery
- Reduces muscle soreness

### Injury Prevention
- Start gradually and progress slowly
- Use proper form - quality over quantity
- Rest between workout days
- Listen to your body - pain is a warning sign
- Stay hydrated during exercise

## Modifications for Common Injuries

### Knee Issues
- Avoid high-impact exercises
- Focus on low-impact cardio (swimming, cycling)
- Strengthen supporting muscles
- Avoid deep squats and lunges initially

### Lower Back Pain
- Avoid heavy lifting initially
- Focus on core strengthening
- Use proper form for all exercises
- Consider physical therapy

### Shoulder Issues
- Avoid overhead movements initially
- Focus on range of motion exercises
- Strengthen rotator cuff muscles
- Modify push-up variations

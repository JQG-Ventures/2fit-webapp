# Beginner Workout Guide

## Getting Started

Starting a fitness journey can be overwhelming. This guide provides safe, effective workouts for beginners.

## First Week Strategy

### Day 1: Full Body Introduction
- Warm-up: 5 minutes light walking
- Bodyweight squats: 2 sets of 8 reps
- Modified push-ups (knee or wall): 2 sets of 5 reps
- Plank: 2 sets of 15 seconds
- Cool-down: 5 minutes stretching

### Day 2: Rest or Light Activity
- 20-minute walk
- Gentle stretching

### Day 3: Full Body (Repeat Day 1)
- Same exercises, try to complete all reps
- Focus on form over speed

### Day 4: Rest

### Day 5: Full Body
- Increase to 3 sets if comfortable
- Add 5 more seconds to plank holds

## Week 2-4 Progression

### Full Body Workout (3x per week)
- Warm-up: 5-10 minutes
- Bodyweight squats: 3 sets x 10 reps
- Push-ups (modified): 3 sets x 8 reps
- Lunges: 2 sets x 8 per leg
- Plank: 3 sets x 20-30 seconds
- Cool-down: 5-10 minutes

### Cardio Days (2x per week)
- 20-30 minutes of walking, cycling, or swimming
- Maintain moderate intensity
- Can talk but slightly breathless

## Beginner Strength Exercises

### Squats
- Stand with feet shoulder-width apart
- Lower as if sitting in a chair
- Keep knees behind toes
- Push through heels to stand
- Start: 2 sets x 8 reps

### Push-ups (Modified)
- Start on knees or against wall
- Lower body with control
- Push back up
- Keep core engaged
- Start: 2 sets x 5 reps

### Plank
- Start on hands and knees
- Extend one leg, then the other
- Keep body straight
- Don't let hips sag
- Start: 15-20 seconds

### Lunges
- Step forward with one leg
- Lower back knee toward ground
- Push back to start
- Alternate legs
- Start: 2 sets x 6 per leg

## Progression Tips

1. **Week 1-2**: Focus on learning form
2. **Week 3-4**: Increase reps or sets
3. **Month 2**: Add more exercises or increase difficulty
4. **Month 3**: Consider adding light weights or resistance bands

## Common Beginner Mistakes

1. **Too much too soon**: Start slow, build gradually
2. **Skipping warm-up/cool-down**: Essential for safety
3. **Poor form**: Quality over quantity always
4. **Not resting**: Muscles need recovery time
5. **Comparing to others**: Your journey is unique

## When to Progress

You're ready to progress when:
- Current workout feels easy
- You can complete all sets with good form
- You're not excessively sore
- You've been consistent for 2-3 weeks

## Rest and Recovery

- Rest 1-2 days between strength workouts
- Sleep 7-9 hours per night
- Stay hydrated
- Eat balanced meals
- Listen to your body

## Safety Reminders

- Stop if you feel sharp pain
- Consult doctor before starting if you have health concerns
- Start with bodyweight before adding resistance
- Focus on form, not speed
- Warm up and cool down every session

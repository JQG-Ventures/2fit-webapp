# Nutrition Basics for Fitness

## Macronutrients Overview

### Protein
- Essential for muscle repair and growth
- Recommended: 0.8-1.2g per pound of body weight for active individuals
- Sources: Lean meat, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, tofu
- Distribute throughout the day
- Important post-workout for recovery

### Carbohydrates
- Primary energy source for workouts
- Complex carbs: Whole grains, sweet potatoes, quinoa, oats
- Simple carbs: Fruits, honey (best around workouts)
- Timing: More carbs on active days
- Quality matters: Choose whole, unprocessed sources

### Fats
- Essential for hormone production and vitamin absorption
- Healthy sources: Avocado, nuts, seeds, olive oil, fatty fish
- Recommended: 20-30% of daily calories
- Avoid trans fats
- Include in every meal for satiety

## Meal Timing for Fitness

### Pre-Workout (1-2 hours before)
- Light meal with carbs and protein
- Examples: Banana with nut butter, oatmeal, yogurt
- Avoid heavy, fatty meals
- Stay hydrated

### Post-Workout (within 30-60 minutes)
- Protein + carbohydrates
- Examples: Protein shake, chicken with rice, Greek yogurt with fruit
- Helps with muscle recovery
- Replenishes glycogen stores

### General Meal Planning
- Eat every 3-4 hours
- Include protein in each meal
- Balance with vegetables
- Stay hydrated throughout the day

## Nutrition by Goal

### Weight Loss
- Moderate calorie deficit (500-750 kcal/day)
- Higher protein to preserve muscle
- Focus on whole, nutrient-dense foods
- Reduce processed foods and added sugars
- Stay hydrated (can help with satiety)
- Don't skip meals - maintain metabolism

### Muscle Gain
- Calorie surplus (300-500 kcal/day)
- Higher protein intake
- Adequate carbohydrates for energy
- Eat around workouts
- Consistent meal timing
- Quality over quantity

### Endurance
- Adequate carbohydrates for energy
- Balanced macronutrients
- Hydration is critical
- Electrolytes for long sessions
- Recovery nutrition important
- Pre and during workout fueling

## Hydration

### Daily Needs
- General: 2-3 liters of water
- Active individuals: 3-4 liters
- More in hot weather or intense training

### Signs of Dehydration
- Thirst
- Dark urine
- Fatigue
- Headaches
- Reduced performance

### Electrolytes
- Important for long workouts (>1 hour)
- Sources: Sports drinks, coconut water, electrolyte tablets
- Balance with water intake

## Meal Planning Tips

### Preparation
- Plan meals for the week
- Prep ingredients in advance
- Cook in batches
- Have healthy snacks ready

### Portion Control
- Use hand measurements:
  - Protein: Palm-sized
  - Carbs: Cupped hand
  - Fats: Thumb-sized
  - Vegetables: Fist-sized

### Balanced Plate
- 1/2 vegetables
- 1/4 protein
- 1/4 complex carbohydrates
- Small amount of healthy fats

## Common Nutrition Mistakes

1. **Skipping meals**: Slows metabolism
2. **Too restrictive**: Not sustainable
3. **Ignoring protein**: Important for recovery
4. **Not hydrating enough**: Affects performance
5. **Overeating "healthy" foods**: Calories still matter
6. **Eliminating food groups**: Usually unnecessary

## Supplements

### When to Consider
- Protein powder: If struggling to meet protein needs
- Multivitamin: If diet is limited
- Vitamin D: If limited sun exposure
- Omega-3: If not eating fatty fish regularly

### Not Necessary
- Most people don't need supplements
- Whole foods are best
- Consult healthcare provider before starting

## Special Considerations

### Vegetarian/Vegan
- Ensure adequate protein from plant sources
- Combine complementary proteins
- Consider B12 and iron supplementation
- Include variety of plant foods

### Food Allergies/Intolerances
- Find suitable alternatives
- Read labels carefully
- Plan meals in advance
- Consult dietitian if needed

## Sustainable Habits

- Focus on whole foods
- Cook at home when possible
- Allow flexibility for social occasions
- Don't label foods as "good" or "bad"
- Progress over perfection
- Consistency is key

Remember: Nutrition is highly individual. What works for one person may not work for another. Experiment, track, and adjust based on your goals and how you feel.
